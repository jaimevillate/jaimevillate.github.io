Introduction to Astrodynamics

Open Training Course
Jaime E. Villate. University of Porto, Portugal

Home→ Courses→ Introduction to Astrodynamics

Any professional or University of Porto student interested in this course can apply for it at the Faculty of Engineering.

Objectives

You will learn the relevant mathematical techniques to determine the orbit of celestial bodies, satellites and spacecrafts under the effect of the gravitational force, and how to compute the position and velocity of those objects at different times. After taking this course, you should be able to explain the foundations of astrodynamics, also known as orbital mechanics.

Program

  1. Coordinates systems and reference frames used to describe orbits.
  2. Motion inside a central force field.
  3. Two-body problem with initial conditions.
  4. Two-body problem with boundary conditions.
  5. Many-body problems.
  6. Interplanetary flight.
  7. Orbit perturbation.
