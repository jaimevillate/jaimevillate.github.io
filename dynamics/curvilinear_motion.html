Introduction

The strong accelerations experienced in a roller coaster are not only due to the changes of the speed, but also to the curved trajectory. The rate of change of the speed is only one of the components of the acceleration, namely, the tangential component. The other component of the acceleration depends on the curvature of the trajectory as it is shown in this chapter.

Note that there are two different tangential unit vectors at point P. One of them is tangent to the curve between B and P and the other is tangent to the curve between P and Q.
